Emily Clare Elliott, Journalist and copy editor

I am a recent graduate of the Master of Journalism program at Toronto Metropolitan University, and I also hold an undergraduate degree in Global Development Studies at Queen’s University.

During my education at Queen’s University, I studied a wide range of topics, including social justice, economics, Indigenous studies, and political sciences. In my second year, I was selected to partake in an enrichment program studying Cuban culture and society at the University of Havana. Throughout my degree, I gained skills and insights that helped me to look at the world through a critical lens. I had the privilege to engage in important topics with courses such as “AIDS, Power, and Poverty,” “Global Mental Health,” “Cities and Urbanization in the South,” and the “Political Economy of Resource Extraction.” My research interests included the prevalence of HIV/AIDS among undocumented Haitian workers in the Dominican Republic, the preservation of Indigenous cultures, languages, and traditions, and volunteer tourism through the popular humanitarian gaze.

Throughout my graduate degree, I learned the basics as well as the complexities of producing journalism. I engaged in a variety of topics ranging from the laws and ethics associated with journalism to the key essentials needed to create and publish a magazine. As per degree requirements, I streamlined into the narrative program in which I assisted in the production of the annual issue of the Review of Journalism. In the role of Senior Editor, I oversaw the writing, editing, and layout of all front-of-book and back-of-book stories. In addition, I authored a narrative featured in the magazine entitled “Rage Against the Machines,” which reported on the media coverage surrounding the “Freedom Convoy” that occurred in Ottawa in February 2022.

With the guidance of professor Bill Reynolds at Toronto Metropolitan University, my peers and I launched The Otter, a publication featuring longform narratives. As I have a passion for meticulous detail, I was selected to be the chief copy editor for the new online magazine.

Currently, I am based in Kingston, Ontario working as a freelance journalist.
